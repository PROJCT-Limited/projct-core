Governance

Grievance Handling

PROJCT is committed to providing an accessible and transparent way for stakeholders to raise concerns or grievances about our social, environmental, or governance practices.

How to raise a concern

Submitting a Grievance

Concerns can be submitted via direct email to ops@projct.co. All communications are treated confidentially.

Grounds for Acceptance

A grievance is accepted if it relates to our operations, including but not limited to: health and safety, environmental impacts, labour conditions, human rights, unethical behaviour, or breaches of company policies.

If a grievance is not accepted (e.g., it is out of scope or frivolous), PROJCT will inform the complainant in writing of the specific reasons for rejection.

Process

All grievances are logged, assessed for urgency and impact, and a relevant team member is assigned responsibility for a response and resolution.

  • Acknowledgement of receipt within 2 business days
  • Resolution provided within 10 business days
  • Regular updates communicated to the relevant stakeholder throughout the process

Facilitating Resolution

Resolution is facilitated through investigation, direct dialogue, mediation, or corrective actions (such as policy change or apology) aimed at addressing the root cause.

Protections

Confidentiality & Non-Retaliation

We strictly prohibit any form of retaliation against stakeholders who raise grievances in good faith. The identity of the complainant and details of the grievance will be kept confidential, sharing information only on a need-to-know basis for the purposes of investigation.

Enforcement

Any employee or manager found to be retaliating — including bullying, adverse treatment, or termination — will face disciplinary action.

Reporting Retaliation

If a stakeholder feels they are being retaliated against, they should immediately report it to the Managing Director.
